Opis Problemu (The exam-scheduling project)

The project concerns allocating (scheduling) examinations into the smallest number of pre-defined time-slots in a way that avoids exam conflicts; i.e. any student would not be required to take exams that are scheduled in the same time slot.
Formally, the exam scheduling problem can be described as follows:

  • there are N students
  • there are M exams to schedule into separate time-slots
  • there are S time-slots
  • every student takes “L(k)” exams (“k” denotes a specific student from among all students and “L(k)” denotes a student-specific list of exams)

The problem is to schedule M exams into S time-slots so as to enable all students to take their exams.

Note1: If there was no pre-defined number of time-slots the problem could be solved by allocating each exam to a separate time-slot. However, such a solution is not practical as it would imply a very long examination session.

Note2: If the number of time-slots is too small the exam-scheduling problem may not have a solution. For example if there was just one time-slot the exam- scheduling problem would not have a solution if there was even one student who took 2 exams.

With a number of time-slots that is greater than the minimum required for the solution of the exam-scheduling problem one can strive to refine the allocation of exams to time-slots in a way that creates greatest gaps between exams for individual students. For students sitting two exams s time-slots apart, the approximate costs are ws i.e. w0=16, w1=8, w2=4, w3=2 and w4=1. The overall quality of the exam schedule can be measured as a sum of the costs ws averaged for all students.

For example if a student had exams scheduled in time-slots: 1, 2, 4, 8, the cost evaluated for this student’s exam schedule would be 16+8+2=26.

Similar evaluation would be done for all students’ exam schedules and averaged over the number of students.

The enclosed file sta-f-83-stu.txt provides a list of exams taken by 611 students (exams are labeled between 0001 and 0139).

Project tasks:

  • Review the literature on exam scheduling
  • Select and justify your approach
  • Implement your selected approach to exam scheduling and test the program on the data provided (assume that there are 14 time slots available)
  • Evaluate the quality of the exam schedule.
